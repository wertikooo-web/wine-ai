Roast lamb has a rich, slightly gamey flavor and enough fat to stand up to a wine with real structure and tannin. From Moldovan wines, a good match is a red from the Ștefan Vodă or Purcari region built around Fetească Neagră blended with Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot — the tannins cut through the fat, and dark-fruit notes complement the roasted, herbal character of the lamb.

A single-varietal Cabernet Sauvignon from Moldova is also a safe, reliable pairing for roast lamb: its firm tannin structure and black-currant character work the same way classic Bordeaux-style reds do with lamb.

For a lighter take on the same dish (herb-crusted, less fatty preparation), a medium-bodied Fetească Neagră on its own can work well without overpowering the meat.
