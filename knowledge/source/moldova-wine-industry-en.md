Wine is one of Moldova's most economically important products: producers generated over 5% of national exports (5.2% in 2018), wine manufacturing accounted for 15.2% of the food-and-beverage industry and about 6.1% of total industrial output value, and the sector supports an estimated 200,000 people across its full value chain (about 11 out of every 1,000 jobs in the national economy come directly from wineries). Moldova ranks 1st in the world for vineyard density per capita (about 3.44 ha per 100 inhabitants), 14th in the world by vineyard area (133,000 ha, 1.8% of world vineyard surface), 21st in the world for grape production (~720,000 tonnes in 2018) and 19th in the world / 11th in Europe for wine volume produced (about 1.9 million hectolitres in 2018).

At year-end 2018, 187 winemaking units were registered with the Vine and Wine Register (VWR), of which about 110 report regularly to the National Bureau of Statistics and are considered the active core of the industry. Geographically, 51% of wineries are located in the Central region (home to Cricova, Mileştii Mici and Brăneşti, known for large-scale storage/aging capacity), 30% in the Southern region (Bostavan, Fautor, Vinia-Traian, DK Intertrade) and 19% in the South-Eastern region (Purcari, Suvorov-Vin, Sălcuţa). Most wineries (about 75%) are locally privately owned; about 22% (25 wineries) are wholly or partly foreign-owned, including Purcari, DK-Intertrade, Bulgari Winery and Barza Neagră Group (wholly foreign-owned) and Bostavan, Vinia Traian, Château Vartely, Kazayak-Vin and Dionysos-Mereni (shared ownership). The state is the sole shareholder in four companies — Cricova, Mileştii Mici, Barza Albă and AROMA (a divin/brandy producer) — and owns 60.4% of Vinuri-Ialoveni.

By size, the sector is dominated by micro, small and medium producers: in 2018, 46% of wineries processed under 1,000 tonnes of grapes each, 47% processed 1,000-10,000 tonnes, and only about 6% qualify as large companies processing over 10,000 tonnes. By product category (2018, out of 110 active companies): 71% produce red wines, 64% white wines, 17% fortified wines (>15% alcohol) and 12% sparkling wines. Cricova is the country's largest sparkling-wine producer; the top 5 sparkling producers hold 94% of that market. Notable red/rosé leaders include Vinăria din Vale, Mold-Nord Făleşti and Bostavan Winery; notable white leaders include Vinăria din Vale, Sălcuţa and Grape Valley.

The National Office of Vine and Wine (NOVW, Romanian: ONVV) was established in 2013 as a public institution under the Ministry of Agriculture, Regional Development and Environment (MARDE), structured as a public-private partnership so it can act with some independence from political interference. It manages the "Wine of Moldova" country brand, promotes exports, oversees PGI/PDO wine categories, and maintains the Vine and Wine Register (VWR) — a mandatory, free registration system (est. 2016-2017) covering all vineyards over 0.15 ha and all winery units, intended to increase traceability and transparency. NOVW's governing bodies are a Director and a 13-member Coordination Council drawn from the Ministry and the regional wine-producer associations (Codru, Ştefan Vodă, Valul lui Traian, Bălţi, Divin, and small-producer/nursery associations). Its funding comes from mandatory contributions by grape and wine producers plus a matching state-budget allocation. The main legal basis for the sector includes the Law on Vine and Wine no. 57 of 2006 and various technical regulations from 2009-2017 covering wine market organization, labeling, planting material and the VWR itself. Wine is legally defined as "a food product obtained exclusively by total or partial alcoholic fermentation of fresh or frozen grapes... or of fresh grape must," classified into four categories: PDO wine, PGI wine, varietal wine (no PDO/PGI), and generic wine.
