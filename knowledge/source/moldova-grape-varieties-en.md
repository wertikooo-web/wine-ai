Moldova's roughly 133,000 hectares of vine plantations for wine grapes are split by origin into three groups: about 85% European/international varieties, 10% Black Sea Basin varieties, and 5% local/indigenous varieties (source: ONVV and National Bureau of Statistics). By colour, white varieties account for around 70% of technical (wine) vine area and red for about 30%, with aromatic varieties covering roughly a third of all plantings. White varieties dominate in the central Codru region; red varieties are more common in the southern regions.

International white varieties grown in Moldova include Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Aligoté, Pinot Gris, Pinot Blanc, Riesling, Traminer, Muscat (Ottonel and others), Silvaner, Müller-Thurgau and Rkatsiteli. International red varieties include Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Pinot Noir, Malbec and Saperavi (plus Gamay). More recently registered varieties include Syrah, Cabernet Franc, Petit Verdot, Carignan, Montepulciano, Sémillon, Ugni Blanc and Tempranillo.

**Fetească Albă** is a traditional Romanian/Moldovan white variety grown in most of the country's vineyards, producing dry or semi-dry wines with moderate alcohol (11.5-12%), fine floral and fresh flavours, and one of the highest natural sugar accumulations of any variety worldwide (typically 180-220 g/l of must, sometimes 240-270 g/l when over-ripened). Wineries producing it include Cricova, Château Vartely, Vinăria din Vale, Equinox, Carpe Diem and Mimi.

**Fetească Regală** is a white variety created by crossing Fetească Albă with Grasă de Cotnari, introduced after 1920. It has medium vigor, high fertility (80-85% fertile shoots), moderate frost resistance but drought sensitivity, and produces dry, fresh, elegant wines (10-12.5% alcohol) with wild-flower, dried-apricot and almond notes. Wineries producing it include Cricova, Château Vartely, Gitana, Vinăria Dacă, Equinox and Carpe Diem.

**Fetească Neagră** is an old indigenous red variety originating from the Prut river valley — vigorous, frost- and drought-resistant, ripening from around 10 September and accumulating 200-260 g/l sugar. Wines range from bright ruby to intense red with complex berry and sweet-cherry aromas and a long fruity finish; quality improves with 2-3 years of oak aging and up to 10-12 years of bottle aging. Alcohol content is typically 12-12.5%, in dry, semi-dry or sweet styles. Producers include Cricova, Château Vartely and Carpe Diem.

**Rară Neagră** (known as Băbească Neagră in Romania) is a rare indigenous variety cultivated since Geto-Dacian times, best suited to the warm southern slopes of Moldova; it is very fertile with a strong root system and drought resistance, ripening in the second half of September. Its wines are elegant ruby-coloured with pomegranate notes, a full velvety texture, and dried-fruit/vanilla character; they are considered difficult to mature but highly sought after and among Moldova's most expensive wines. Rară Neagră is the key blending component of Negru de Purcari, Moldova's most famous wine. Other producers include Cricova, Mileştii Mici, Equinox and Carlevana.

**Viorica** is a more recently registered variety, well adapted to Moldova's climate — it produced the largest harvest of any variety during the 2012 drought. Cricova winery has been actively producing Viorica wines in recent years. Other indigenous/local varieties mentioned alongside these include Fetească Regală, Plavaie and Buşuioacă Albă. Local varieties collectively cover only about 5% of total vine area but are considered central to Moldovan wine's distinct national identity and are expanding due to growing international interest and government support programs.
